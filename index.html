According to Mister Li, on the nueral workings of percieving rhythm,

the sound signals goes through the ear and the vestibulocochlear nerve to reach the

BRAINSTEM!!!!!

in which there are "central pattern generators" (CPGs) which drive rhythmic output.

these CPGs drive the internal metronome,

which are synchronized to external cycles of music via RhYthMic EnTraiNMent

and I'd like you to keep in mind, that I am literally copying word for word

everything Dany Li is saying in this YouTube video.

so let's learn more about this rhythmic entrainment.

there are five stages of it:

neuronal, in which neurons fire in phase with the external signal (phase locking)

perceptual, when we begin to expect and anticipate beats,

autonomic, in which our unconscious biological systems like heart rate and respiration try to synchronize to the rate of the music.

(high heart rate when I listen to rap music)

motor, which involves conscious physical movement after perceiving a beat

and social, which includes us socially entraining to others in the form of conversation 1

Now Danny Li is talking about rhythm deficiency in Parkinson's Disease, when there is a decreased dopamine output due to degeneration of the substantia nigra, and thus impaired motor function: "people with Parkinson's Disease also have a lot of trouble with their internal metronomes, with their rhythm...there have been attempts to use external rhythmic stimulus to help treat some of the symptoms of Parkinson's disease."

such as listening to a metronome ticking to try to entrain to the external stimulus.
